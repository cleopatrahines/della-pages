Before You Buy

What to Know Before Choosing a Mini Split for an Old House

Check your electrical panel first. Most mini splits need a dedicated 115V or 230V circuit.
Insulation still matters. Better insulation usually means a smaller, quieter, less expensive system.
Plan the line-set route early. Older walls, plaster, and trim may require a cleaner surface-run strategy.
For most old houses, start with one room first. Move to 2-zone or 3-zone only when more rooms need independent control, and use concealed ducted when visible wall heads are the issue.
Common Questions

Mini Split FAQ for Old Houses

Quick answers before you buy.

What mini split system works best for an old house?

The best path for an old house usually starts with room count. Many buyers begin with single-zone for one difficult room, then move to 2-zone or 3-zone if more rooms need help. Concealed ducted is the secondary option when a cleaner look matters more.

Are mini splits good for old houses?

Yes. Mini splits are one of the best HVAC options for old houses because they add heating and cooling without rebuilding full-house ductwork. You can start with single-zone for one room, step up to 2-zone or 3-zone for more rooms, and use concealed ducted when you want a cleaner look.

Should I start with single-zone or go straight to 2-zone?

Start with single-zone if one room is clearly the main problem. Go straight to 2-zone when two rooms on the same floor both need regular heating and cooling and you want separate control in each room.

When does 3-zone make sense in an old house?

3-zone makes sense when three connected rooms need independent comfort and you want one outdoor unit instead of stacking multiple separate systems. It is usually a better fit for larger upstairs or whole-floor retrofits.

When should I choose concealed ducted instead of wall-mounted?

Choose concealed ducted when you want less visible equipment and have a closet, attic, soffit, or ceiling cavity that can hide the air handler. Wall-mounted is still the easier and more common first option in many old houses.

Can you install a mini split in an old house without existing ductwork?

Yes. Many mini split retrofits in old houses work without existing ductwork. Single-zone, 2-zone, and 3-zone systems do not require a full traditional duct system, while concealed ducted setups can use short hidden runs when the layout calls for them.
