No Existing Ducts
Most pre-1960s homes were built without forced-air ductwork.

Radiator-Era Layouts
Rooms were designed around radiators and baseboards, not central air.

Before You Buy

What to Know First

Check your electrical panel. Most mini splits need a dedicated 115V or 230V circuit
Insulation still matters. Better insulation usually means a smaller, quieter system
Plan where the line set will run. Older walls may need surface routing
Start with single-zone for one problem room, then expand to multi-zone if needed
All DELLA mini splits include a wall-mount bracket, remote, and installation kit
Common Questions

Mini Split for Old House FAQ

Quick answers before you buy.

What is the best mini split type for an old house?

For most older homes, a single-zone wall-mounted mini split is the easiest place to start. It works without ductwork, installs with minimal disruption, and lets you solve one problem room before expanding further.

Are mini splits good for old houses?

Yes. Mini splits are one of the best HVAC options for older homes because they add heating and cooling without rebuilding the house around ductwork.

What works best in a home with radiators or baseboards?

A floor-ceiling mini split is often the best fit when low-wall placement makes more sense. If you still have enough high-wall space, a wall-mounted unit can also work well.

When should I choose concealed ducted instead?

Choose concealed ducted when you want a cleaner interior look and have space to hide the unit in a closet, attic, or ceiling area.

Should I choose multi-zone or separate single-zone systems?

For 2–3 rooms, a multi-zone system is usually the cleaner option. For rooms that are far apart or on different floors, separate single-zone systems can be simpler to install.

Can you install a mini split in a house without ductwork?

Yes. That is exactly what mini splits are designed for. They only need a small wall opening for the line set, not a full duct system.
