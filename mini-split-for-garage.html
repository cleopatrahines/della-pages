Before You Size

The Garage Checklist

Insulate First

The garage door is the biggest heat leak. Insulate it before sizing — you may need a smaller (cheaper) unit than you think.

Uninsulated? Size Up

No insulation means faster heat loss. Move up one BTU tier to compensate, or you'll run the system harder and pay more in energy.

Cold Climate? Go Ultra Heat

Below freezing regularly? Choose an Ultra Heat system rated to −13°F so the unit doesn't lose capacity when you need it most.

Don't Oversize

Too many BTUs short-cycles the compressor — the garage stays damp and the unit wears out faster. Match the capacity to the space.

Mount High, Aim Smart

Install on the longest wall, high up for widest coverage. Aim toward the work area but not directly at people.

Dedicated Circuit Required

Most units need a dedicated 115V or 230V circuit. Don't share with shop tools — voltage drops damage compressors.

Mini Split FAQ for Garage Buyers

Quick answers to the garage sizing, insulation, and install questions that slow buyers down.

Is a ductless mini split good for a garage?

Yes. No ductwork needed, it mounts on the wall, and it only runs when you use the space. A mini split is one of the most practical ways to heat and cool a garage without tearing into existing structure.

What size mini split do I need for a 2-car garage?

12,000–18,000 BTU covers most 2-car garages. Size up if you have poor insulation, high ceilings, or live in an extreme climate zone. A well-insulated 400 sq ft garage can often start at 12K.

Can a mini split heat an uninsulated garage?

It can, but the garage will gain and lose heat much faster, which makes comfort harder to hold and can push the right BTU higher. Insulating walls and the garage door first gives you better results at a lower capacity tier.

How much does a garage mini split cost?

$700–$1,100 for the unit depending on BTU, efficiency rating, and cold climate features. Installation costs vary by region but a dedicated circuit and basic line-set run keep most garage projects straightforward.

What is the best mini split for a workshop garage?

18,000 BTU handles most workshop garages. Choose a high-efficiency unit if you run it daily, and consider adding a mesh pre-filter if the space generates sawdust or metal particles.

Stop Guessing Your Garage Size

Garages run hotter in summer, colder in winter, and punish the wrong BTU faster than most rooms.
