Before You Buy

What to Know First

Insulate first — the garage door is the biggest source of heat loss
Uninsulated? Size up one BTU tier to compensate
Below freezing? Choose an Ultra Heat system rated to −13°F
Avoid oversizing — it short cycles and leaves the garage damp
Factor in sq ft + insulation + climate, not just floor area

Installation & Maintenance

Placement, Maintenance, and Installation

Where to Mount

Mount high on the longest wall for widest coverage.
Aim toward the work area but not directly at people.
Keep the unit away from dust-heavy zones.
Choose a spot that allows a gravity drain line.

Owner Tips

1
Pre-condition 15–20 min before starting work.
2
Clean filters every 2–3 weeks in dusty shops.
3
Insulate the garage door first — it's the biggest heat loss.
4
Keep the outdoor unit clear of debris and snow.

Before Installation

Gravity Drain vs Condensate Pump

Use gravity drainage when possible. Only add a pump if you can't route a downhill line.

Dust and Filter Care

Add a mesh pre-filter for woodworking shops. Clean it regularly to maintain airflow.

When to DIY vs Hire a Pro

A pro handles refrigerant and code. DIY needs a vacuum pump and line set experience.

Electrical Circuit Check

Most units need a dedicated 115V or 230V circuit. Don't share with shop tools.

Common Questions

Garage Mini Split FAQ

Quick answers before you buy.

Is a ductless mini split good for a garage?

Yes. No ductwork needed and it only runs when you use the space.

What size mini split do I need for a 2-car garage?

12,000–18,000 BTU. Size up for poor insulation or extreme climate.

Can a mini split heat an uninsulated garage?

It can, but costs more. Insulate first for better results.

How much does a garage mini split cost?

$700–$1,100 depending on BTU, efficiency, and cold climate features.

What is the best mini split for a workshop?

18,000 BTU handles most workshops. Choose high-efficiency if you run it daily.
