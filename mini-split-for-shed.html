Before You Buy

The Detached Checklist

Before you lock in 9K, 12K, or 18K, check the detached-building basics that change the load fastest.

01

Power Supply

Confirm the shed can support the right voltage path first. Electrical access often decides whether the project stays simple.

02

Insulation Integrity

Thin walls and simple roof assemblies can push a small shed up one capacity tier faster than expected.

03

Line-Set Path

Plan the wall penetration, drain route, and condenser placement before you commit to the system size.

04

Seasonal Use

A light hobby shed and a full backyard office should not be sized as if they are the same project.

Quick Answers

Mini Split FAQ for Shed Buyers

Quick answers before you buy.

Should I choose 9K or 12K for a shed?

Start with 9K for a compact enclosed shed used lightly and insulated reasonably well. Move to 12K when the shed acts more like a backyard office, runs daily, takes stronger sun, or has weaker insulation.

Do I need 115V or 230V for a shed mini split?

Not always the same answer. Some smaller 9K systems are available in 115V options, but many 12K and larger systems are more commonly paired with 208/230V service. Always confirm voltage on the exact model before you buy.

Can I run a mini split in an uninsulated shed?

You can, but the shed will gain and lose heat much faster, which makes comfort harder to hold and can push the right BTU higher. Insulation is one of the first things to check before sizing up.

Can I install a mini split in a detached shed myself?

Some homeowners do, especially with smaller wall-mounted systems, but the detached building still needs a clean electrical plan, line-set route, and condenser placement. DIY-friendly does not remove the planning work.

Can a mini split heat and cool a shed year-round?

Yes, a heat-pump mini split can handle both heating and cooling for a shed. The key is matching the BTU to the real load and making sure insulation, electrical service, and the install path are ready for year-round use.

Start With 12K if Your Shed Feels Like a Real Room

Most backyard office sheds start with 12K. If the shed stays smaller and lighter-use, move down to 9K. If the load is clearly bigger or rougher, move up to 18K.
