Mini Split FAQ for Apartment Buyers

Quick answers to the apartment sizing and install questions that slow buyers down.

What size mini split do I need for an apartment?

Most apartment mini split projects start at 12,000 BTU, which covers studios and main living areas up to 550 sq ft. A 9,000 BTU mini split handles smaller apartment bedrooms and offices up to 400 sq ft. For larger open-plan apartments or top-floor units, 18,000 BTU covers up to 1,000 sq ft. Size by your main room, not total apartment square footage.

Is a mini split better than a window AC for an apartment?

For most apartments, yes. An apartment mini split runs at 28-35 decibels versus 50-60 for a window AC, delivers higher efficiency at 17-25 SEER2 versus 10-15 EER, and does not block your window or create a security gap. The trade-off is higher upfront cost and a permanent install that needs building approval.

How quiet is a mini split in an apartment?

A mini split for apartment use typically runs between 28-35 decibels indoors, quieter than a whisper. The compressor sits in the outdoor unit, so the noise stays outside. This matters in apartment buildings where shared walls and close neighbors make window AC noise harder to live with.

Can you install a mini split in an apartment building?

Often yes. Many apartment buildings allow mini split installation, but you need building approval first. The outdoor unit needs a balcony, exterior wall bracket, or ground-level pad. The indoor and outdoor units connect through a small 3-inch hole in the wall. Confirm placement rules, electrical access, and line-set routing with your landlord or HOA before ordering.

Where does the outdoor unit go in an apartment?

For an apartment mini split, common outdoor unit placements include a balcony, an exterior wall bracket, or a ground-level concrete pad. The unit connects to the indoor head through a small line-set routed through the wall. Building rules, floor level, and exterior access determine which option works for your apartment.

Can a renter install a mini split in an apartment?

It depends on your lease and landlord. An apartment mini split is a permanent install that adds value to the unit, so some landlords approve it. You will need written permission and the install must follow building codes. If your landlord says no, a portable AC is the only non-permanent apartment cooling alternative.
